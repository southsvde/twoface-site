MP3 vs WAV — The Difference & Why It Matters

  • WAV = studio master. Uncompressed, full detail (16/24-bit). Clean vocals, clean mix, loud master.
  • MP3 = listening copy. It removes info to shrink the file; artifacts get louder when you process it.
  • No double-compression. Start with WAV so streaming platforms don’t re-encode an MP3 again.
  • Future-proof. Engineers, distributors, and sync libraries expect WAV. You can always make an MP3 later.
