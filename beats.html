MP3 vs WAV — Why upgrade?

  • WAV = full quality (lossless). MP3 is compressed and removes detail you’ll hear when stacking vocals or mixing.
  • Headroom for mixing/mastering. WAV preserves transients and low-end accuracy for cleaner processing.
  • Distribution-ready. Labels and engineers prefer WAV to avoid generational loss.

Recommendation: choose MP3 + WAV to write with the MP3 and record/release with the WAV.
